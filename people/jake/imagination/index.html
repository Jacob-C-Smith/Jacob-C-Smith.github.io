Knowledge

"There can be no doubt that all our knowledge begins with experience." - Immanuel Kant

Computer Science is valuable because it can augment ones' knowledge. Knowledge is created when a subject observes an object in action. The impressions left on the senses of the subject are some set of the qualities of the object. The object appears, so to speak, twice. The first time as a material. The second time as an ideal. As a subject observes objects, the subject will begin to build knowledge. The building of knowledge is a two step cycle. First, material objects are compared against the ideal. The material objects are classified according to their shared qualities. This creates a new ideal, that is applicable to each instance of the material. A subsequent deductive process compares the ideal to the material. Knowledge is refined when one finds that the rational ideal is incongruent with the material reality.
